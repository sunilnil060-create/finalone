About This Project

Partnership & Funding

The Highland Heritages of Bhutan portal was made possible through an international research partnership, supported by the Gerda Henkel Foundation — bringing together academic excellence, community trust, and cultural stewardship across the Himalayan highlands.

How It Began

A Grant, a Partnership, and a Shared Purpose

In 2022, Royal Thimphu College and Leiden University came together around a shared conviction — that the oral traditions, cultural practices, and living knowledge of Bhutan's highland communities deserved careful, community-centred documentation before they were lost to time.

The Gerda Henkel Foundation, one of Europe's leading private foundations for the promotion of humanities research, awarded funding that made this collaboration possible. Their support enabled field research across three highland locations — Haa, Laya, and Sephu — over a period of two years.

What emerged was not only an archive, but a method: one that placed community voice at the centre, and treated the act of documentation itself as a form of cultural respect.

Institutional Partners

Three Organisations, One Archive

Each partner brought a distinct expertise — fieldwork, scholarship, and academic infrastructure — that together made this project possible.

Lead Institution · Bhutan

Royal Thimphu College

Royal Thimphu College is Bhutan's first autonomous college affiliated with the Royal University of Bhutan. Located in Thimphu, RTC is a liberal arts institution committed to academic excellence, community-engaged research, and the preservation of Bhutanese cultural heritage.

RTC led the field research, community engagement, and documentation across all three highland locations, working directly with local communities to ensure that voices were heard, respected, and preserved with care.

Visit RTC
Academic Partner · Netherlands

Leiden University

Founded in 1575, Leiden University is one of Europe's leading research universities and the oldest university in the Netherlands. Its Faculty of Humanities has a long tradition of scholarship in Asian studies, ethnography, and the documentation of living cultural traditions.

Leiden provided the academic framework, comparative research methodology, and international scholarly network that shaped the project's approach to documentation, archiving, and ethical field practice.

Visit Leiden University
Gerda Henkel Foundation
Principal Funder · Germany

Gerda Henkel Foundation

The Gerda Henkel Foundation is a private, independent foundation based in Düsseldorf, Germany, established in 1976. It is one of the most significant private foundations in Europe for the promotion of the humanities, supporting research in history, archaeology, art history, and cultural heritage.

The Foundation's grant made this entire project possible — covering two years of fieldwork, documentation, community engagement, and the development of this digital archive. Their commitment to endangered heritage research aligned closely with the goals of this collaboration.

Project Timeline

How the Collaboration Unfolded

2021

Partnership Formed

Royal Thimphu College and Leiden University establish a formal research partnership, identifying the highland communities of Haa, Laya, and Sephu as primary field sites for collaborative documentation work.

2022

Gerda Henkel Grant Awarded

The Gerda Henkel Foundation awards a research grant to the partnership, providing the funding needed to begin two years of sustained field research, community engagement, and multimedia documentation across the three highland locations.

2022 – 2024

Field Research & Documentation

RTC researchers and students, in collaboration with Leiden scholars, conduct field visits across Haa, Laya, and Sephu. Films, photo essays, ethnographic vignettes, and audio recordings are gathered across multiple field seasons.

2024

Archive Development

The Highland Heritages of Bhutan portal is developed, bringing together all documented media into a publicly accessible digital archive organised by location, theme, and media format.

2025

Portal Launch

The portal launches publicly, making the archive accessible to students, researchers, educators, and communities across Bhutan and beyond. Lesson plans and teaching resources accompany the archive for use in educational settings.
